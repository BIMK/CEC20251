Call for Papers

——IEEE CEC 2025 Special Session on “Large-scale multi-objective optimization”


Aim

The increasing prevalence of heterogeneous data across diverse scientific and engineering domains presents significant challenges to optimization, demanding both efficiency and effectiveness. These challenges are often compounded by the presence of multiple, conflicting objectives and a large number of decision variables (typically hundreds or more), hindering the ability of algorithms to achieve well-converged and diverse solutions. This class of problem, known as Large-Scale Multi-objective Optimization Problems (LSMOPs), poses a significant hurdle for even advanced evolutionary multi-objective optimization algorithms due to the curse of dimensionality, complex fitness landscapes, and intricate variable linkages, exacerbating the difficulty of achieving satisfactory convergence. Therefore, this special issue seeks contributions focused on novel, practical, and versatile algorithms designed to effectively address the challenges inherent in LSMOPs, ultimately advancing the field of computational intelligence and driving progress in related application areas.


Scope

The topics of this special session include, but are not limited to, the follows:

  • Novel algorithms for solving LSMOPs, e.g., evolutionary algorithms, swarm intelligence algorithms, mathematical programming methods, reinforcement learning methods, and others.
  • Tailored algorithms for solving specific types of LSMOPs, e.g., sparse problems, constrained problems, expensive problems, dynamic problems, multimodal problems, robust problems, and others.
  • Effective algorithms for solving combinatorial LSMOPs, e.g., subset selection problems, vehicle routing problems, hybrid encoding problems, and others.
  • Applications of existing algorithms to LSMOPs in emerging areas, e.g., machine learning, data mining, manufacturing, scheduling, electrics, economics, bioinformatics, medicine, and others.
  • Performance assessment, theoretical analysis, and benchmarking of algorithms for solving LSMOPs.
  • Decomposition strategies and efficient search space exploration techniques for LSMOPs: including novel decomposition methods, dimensionality reduction techniques, surrogate-assisted optimization, and meta-modeling.
  • Handling uncertainty and noise in LSMOPs: including robust optimization methods, stochastic optimization algorithms, and methods for handling noisy or incomplete data.
  • Parallel and distributed computing for LSMOPs: including parallel evolutionary algorithms, distributed optimization methods, and GPU-accelerated algorithms.
  • Effective and efficient algorithms for solving deep learning tasks, e.g., the training of neural networks, network pruning, recommendation, feature selection, network quantization, adversarial attacks, and others.
